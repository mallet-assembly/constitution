Membership and Meetings

Membership

  1. Membership in the Mallet Assembly shall be open to all individuals, without regard to race, ethnicity, religious affliation, sex, gender, sexual orientation, disability status, age, or national origin.
  2. A Member of the Assembly shall be anyone who has been admitted by a majority vote of the Admissions Committee and is in good standing with the Assembly.
  3. An Active Member of the Assembly shall be any Member of the Assembly who owes no fines, fees, or dues to the Assembly, and has paid that semester's dorm dues. Active Membership in the Assembly extends until the deadline for dorm dues of the next semester.
  4. An Ex Officio Member of the Assembly is one who is considered a Member of the Assembly by virtue of occupying a place on the Executive Committee or Board of Governors. An Ex Officio Member is only considered a Member of the Assembly for the duration of their occupation of the office granting that status. They are not and may not become Active Members of the Assembly, except by first becoming a Member of the Assembly through the admissions process.

Meetings

  1. Assembly Meetings are the principle governing meetings of the Assembly, and shall be convened once every two weeks.
  2. A schedule of Assembly Meetings for the semester shall be publicly posted at the beginning of each semester.
  3. The President of the Assembly shall preside over Assembly Meetings. If the President is unavailable, the meeting shall be conducted by the highest ordered officer in attendence as listed in this document. In the absence of all officers of the Assembly, the Assembly shall panic.
  4. Assembly Meetings Assembly shall be open only to Members of the Assembly. No non-Member of the Assembly shall be allowed to speak at or attend an Assembly Meeting, except with special permission of the Executive Committee.
  5. Any Member of the Assembly may speak at Assembly Meetings. Only Active Members may vote at Assembly Meetings. No absentee votes shall be cast.
  6. No dorm meeting shall procede without a quorum of Active Members. A quorum shall be defined as one-third (1/3) of Active Members. The suggestion of a possible absence of a quorum shall be raised immediately upon suspicion thereof. The presiding officer shall make a determination of the existence of a quorum. Failure to suggest an absence of a quorum invalidates all business conducted since the last known presence of a quorum.

Officers

List of Officers

  1. The President shall maintain communication between the Assembly, its officers, and committees and the University Administration, faculty and off-campus organizations. They shall chair all non-committee meetings. They shall see that this Constitution, Bylaws, and all Assembly orders and mandates are promptly, efficiently, and faithfully executed. With the advice of the Executive Committee, the President shall appoint all non-elected committee chairs. storian shall maintain a comprehensive and timely record of the important facts and fictions of the Mallet Assembly's history. He shall chair the Alumni Relations committee and be responsible for setting its agenda and meeting time. They shall serve as a Tribunal member and as an ex officio member of the Board of Governors.
  2. The Vice President shall serve as Chair of the Executive Committee, Chair of the Academic Committee, and as a Tribunal member. They shall coordinate the Mallet Speaker series.
  3. The Third Member of the Tribunal shall preside over the Tribunal and be in charge of its activities and affairs. They shall maintain the official copy of the constitution, bylaws, and record of established precedents. They shall with the advice of the Tribunal, pass judgements about the interpretation of the constitution, bylaws, and established precedents.
  4. The Senior Monitor shall oversee the premises of the Assembly. They shall form room lists and coordinate building and room arrangements with the university. They shall chair the Urban Renewal Committee.
  5. The Minister of Information shall establish and maintain communication between the Assembly's standing and ad hoc committee chairs and other on-campus organizations. They shall keep track of and maintain the Assembly's important documents. They shall ensure that the Assembly is current on all University-mandated paperwork, documents, sites, and other administrivialities. They shall head the Ministry of Information.
  6. The Admissions Committee Chair shall preside at meetings of the Admissions Committee. They shall seek out qualified individuals for admission into the Assembly.
  7. The Scribe shall take minutes of Dorm, Executive Committee, and Tribunal meetings. They shall maintain a record of the minutes of Assembly, Executive Committee, and Tribunal meetings and decisions. They shall be a permanent member of the Ministry of Information.
  8. The Treasurer shall collect, disburse, and account for all Assembly funds. They shall maintain an accurate and complete ledger of Assembly finances. They shall collect all fines. They shall chair the Budget Committee. The Treasurer shall maintain a list of Active Members of the Assembly, and report which members are active to the presiding officer of Assembly Meetings.
  9. The Social Committee Chair shall chair the Social Committee.
  10. The Risk Management Chair shall chair the Risk Management Committee and be a permanent member of the Social Committee.
  11. The Sports Committee Chair shall chair the Sports Committee.
  12. The Service Committee Chair shall chair the Service Committee.
  13. The Historian shall maintain a comprehensive and timely record of the important facts and fictions of the Mallet Assembly's history. They shall chair the Alumni Relations Committee. They shall exercise administrative power over any canonical source of Mallet history.
  14. The Sysop shall chair the Tech Committee.
  15. The Librarian shall maintain and organize the Glenn Hardy Memorial Library. They shall acquire new books and materials, and subscribe to magazines recommended by the Assembly.
  16. The Alumni Representative is elected from the body of the Assembly's alumni. They shall attempt to best represent the interests of the entirety of the Assembly's alumni to the Assembly. They shall be a permanent member of the Alumni Relations Committee. They shall serve as an ex officio member of the Executive Committee and as an ex officio member of the Board of Governors.
